Glossary

Name  Defintion
Acidity Regulators  Food additives used to change or maintain pH. 
Anticaking Agents Keep powders such as milk powder from caking or sticking.
Antifoaming Agents Reduce or prevent foaming in foods.
Antioxidants Compounds that inhibit oxidation, a chemical reaction that can produce free radicals and chain reactions that may damage the cells of organisms.
Antioxidants  Preservatives that serve to inhibit the effects of oxygen on food, and can be beneficial to health.
Bulking Agents Additives that increase the bulk of a food without affecting its nutritional value.
Chemical Abstracts Service number Chemical Abstracts Service (CAS) is a division of the American Chemical Society. It is a source of chemical information.
Codex Alimentarius The Codex Alimentarius is a collection of internationally recognized standards, codes of practice, guidelines, and other recommendations relating to food, food production, food labeling, and food safety.
Color Additive Any dye, pigment or substance that imparts color when it is added to food or drink.
Color Retention Agents used to preserve a food's existing color.
E Numbers Unique number used in Europe for all approved additives.
Emulsifiers An emulsion is a mixture of two or more liquids that are normally immiscible owing to liquid-liquid phase separation.
European Food Safety Authority (EFSA)  Provides independent scientific advice and communicates on existing and emerging risks associated with the food chain.
Flavor Enhancers Enhance a food's existing flavors.
Flavors Additives that give food a particular taste or smell, and may be derived from natural ingredients or created artificially.
Flour Treatment Agents Added to flour to improve its color or its use in baking.
Food Acids Chemical compounds that confer a tart, sour, or acidic flavor to foods.
Food Additive Substances added to food to preserve flavor or enhance taste, appearance, or other sensory qualities. 
Food and Agriculture Organization (FAO)  A specialized agency of the United Nations that leads international efforts to defeat hunger and improve nutrition and food security. 
Food and Drug Administration (FDA or USFDA) A federal agency of the Department of Health and Human Services. The FDA is responsible for protecting and promoting public health through the control and supervision of food safety.
Food Safety A scientific discipline describing handling, preparation, and storage of food in ways that prevent food-borne illness.
Generally Recognized As Safe (GRAS) A United States Food and Drug Administration (FDA) designation that a chemical or substance added to food is considered safe by experts.
Glazing Agents Provide a shiny appearance or protective coating to foods.
Humectants  Prevent foods from drying out.
International Numbering System for Food Additives (INS) A European-based naming system for food additives, aimed at providing a short designation of what may be a lengthy actual name.
Joint FAO/WHO Expert Committee on Food Additives (JEFCA) An international report of the World Health Organization where an Expert Committee evaluates selected food additives and contaminants and establishes acceptable daily intakes for these substances.
Preservatives A substance or a chemical that is added to products such as food products and many other products to prevent decomposition by microbial growth or by undesirable chemical changes.
Stabilizers Thickeners and gelling agents gives foods a firmer texture.
Sweeteners Added to foods for flavoring.
Thickeners A thickening agent or thickener is a substance which can increase the viscosity of a liquid without substantially changing its other properties. 
Tracer Gas Prevent foods from being exposed to atmosphere, thus guaranteeing shelf life.
